# SOCIAL_INVESTIGATION.md — Social and Investigation Rules

> **Purpose**: Rules for social interaction and investigation

---

## 0) Social Interaction Basics

### Key Principles
1. Roleplay > dice when possible
2. NPCs have their own motivations
3. Information is earned, not given freely
4. Lies can be detected with effort

---

## 1) Ability Checks for Social Situations

| Check | Used When |
|-------|-----------|
| **Persuasion** | Convincing someone through reason, charm, or flattery |
| **Deception** | Misleading someone through lies or half-truths |
| **Intimidation** | Coercing someone through threats or force |
| **Performance** | Entertaining or impressing through skill |
| **Insight** | Reading emotions or detecting lies |

### Check Modifiers
```
Persuasion: Charisma + Proficiency (if trained)
Deception: Charisma + Proficiency (if trained)
Intimidation: Strength or Charisma + Proficiency (if trained)
Performance: Charisma + Proficiency (if trained)
Insight: Wisdom + Proficiency (if trained)
```

## 3) Insight Checks

### Using Insight
- Roll to detect deception or read intentions
- Compare against Deception or Performance check
- Only one check per conversation with same NPC

### Insight Results
| Your Roll vs. Their Check | Result |
|---------------------------|--------|
| Your roll ≥ Their roll + 10 | Completely see through |
| Your roll ≥ Their roll + 5 | Clearly suspicious |
| Your roll ≥ Their roll + 0 | No obvious suspicion |
| Your roll < Their roll | Convinced by them |

### What Insight Can Reveal
- Is someone lying?
- Is someone nervous?
- Is someone being threatened?
- Is someone hiding something?

### What Insight Cannot Reveal
- The specific truth
- Magical deception (unless you can detect magic)
- Thoughts (read the mind is different)

## 4) Investigation Rules

### Investigation vs. Perception
- **Perception**: Noticing things passively or actively scanning
- **Investigation**: Actively deducing, researching, or piecing together clues

### Investigation Checks
| Situation | DC |
|-----------|----|
| Obvious clue | 10 |
| Hidden clue | 15 |
| Well-hidden clue | 20 |
| Evidence requiring expertise | 15 + relevant skill |

### Deduction Chain
1. Gather clues (Perception or Investigation)
2. Piece together information (Investigation)
3. Form hypothesis (Investigation)
4. Test hypothesis (may require other checks)

### Evidence Types
| Type | Information Value |
|------|-------------------|
| Physical evidence | Can be examined, may have fingerprints, etc. |
| Documentary evidence | Letters, ledgers, official documents |
| Testimonial evidence | What witnesses say (may be unreliable) |
| Circumstantial evidence | Points to conclusion without proof |

## 5) Interviewing Witnesses

### Witness Cooperation
| Willingness | DC Modifier |
|-------------|-------------|
| Eager to help | -5 |
| Willing | +0 |
| Reluctant | +5 |
| Refusing to talk | +15 or impossible |

### Interview Techniques
| Technique | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Sympathetic approach | +2 to check, better information |
| Aggressive approach | -2 to check, may get false info |
| Bribery | +5 to check, requires payment |
| Authority display | +2 or -5 depending on NPC |

### Taking Notes
- Good investigators take notes
- Notes help with Investigation checks later
- Notes can be lost or stolen

## 6) Gathering Information in Town

### Information Gathering Locations
| Location | Type of Information |
|----------|---------------------|
| Tavern | Rumors, local news, criminal underworld |
| Market | Trade news, travel conditions |
| Church/Temple | Religious matters, charitable work |
| Guard post | Legal matters, official announcements |
| Noble house | High society gossip |
| Shop | Trade information, specialized knowledge |
| Inn | Travelers' news, overnight guests |

### Gathering Information Check
```
DC = 10 for general information
DC = 15 for specific information
DC = 20 for secret or hidden information
```

### Time Required
| Information Type | Time |
|------------------|------|
| General rumors | 1d4 hours |
| Specific but public | 1d6 hours |
| Hidden information | 1d4 days |
| Deep secrets | 2d6 days or more |

## 7) Negotiation

### Negotiation Process
1. Establish starting position
2. Identify interests
3. Generate options
4. Evaluate options
5. Reach agreement

### Negotiation Checks
| Situation | Check | DC |
|-----------|-------|----|
| Basic price | Charisma | 10 + 10% of price |
| Complex deal | Persuasion | 15-25 |
| Intermediary | Performance | 12-18 |
| Breaking deal | Intimidation | 15-25 |

### Negotiation Modifiers
| Factor | Modifier |
|--------|----------|
| Excellent goods | +5 |
| Damaged goods | -5 |
| Buyer's desperation | +5 |
| Seller's desperation | -5 |
| Time pressure | -2 to both sides |

---

## 8) Deception and Lies

### Maintaining a Lie
- Keep track of what you've said
- Be consistent
- Have a backup explanation

### Detecting Lies
| Check | DC |
|-------|----|
| Simple lie | 10 |
| Elaborate lie | 15 |
| Well-practiced lie | 20 |
| Lie to expert | 25 |

### Consequences of Being Caught
| Severity | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| First offense | Warning, NPC becomes suspicious |
| Second offense | NPC refuses further interaction |
| Serious offense | NPC may alert authorities |

## 10) Roleplay Awards

### Awards for Excellent Roleplay
| Behavior | Award |
|----------|-------|
| Clever approach to social situation | Advantage on check |
| In-character dialogue advancing goal | Information bonus |
| Creative use of resources | Reduced DC |
| Good character consistency | DM discretion |

### Awards for Poor Roleplay
| Behavior | Penalty |
|----------|---------|
| Metagaming (using out-of-character knowledge) | Disadvantage on check |
| Inconsistent character behavior | DM discretion |
| Disrupting social flow | Lose opportunity |
