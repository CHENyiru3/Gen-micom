- **Basel Council Ongoing**: Church authority continuously fragmented

## Mist History Timeline (Secret Events)

### 1443 - Night Before Fracture
- **Clement VIII** dies (before winter solstice)
- Deathbed plea: "Let the world witness: what a world without grace looks like"

### 1443 Winter Solstice - Fracture Born
- **Clement VIII** dies that night
- **First Fracture Appears at Jerusalem Temple Mount**
- Father Marcus present, witnesses colorless light and sound of "something being born or dying"

### 1444 - Fracture Period Spreads
- **Spring 1444**: Jerusalem fracture briefly closes then recurs
- **Summer 1444**: Fracture appears in Alexandria, someone attempts blood sacrifice
- **Blood Sacrifice Consequences**: Fracture expands three times, entire street's people die with bleeding eyes
- **Fall 1444**: Fracture appears in Constantinople
- **Around Winter Solstice 1444**: Blue light appears in Nebelheim quarry

## Historical Archives (Optional Background)

### Clement VIII (Gil Sánchez Muñoz y Carbón)

- **Life**: Became antipope after Pisa Council 1417, abdicated 1429, retired to Mallorca
- **Historical Position**: Last antipope to abdicate
- **Mist History Role**: Deathbed plea initiates Fracture Period

### Elijah

- **Research Time**: Ten years fracture research
- **Experience**: Jerusalem, Alexandria, etc.
- **Current Status**: Appears in Nebelheim

### Alexandria Failure Case

- **Time**: Spring 1444
- **Method**: Blood sacrifice (seven children)
- **Result**: Fracture temporarily closes for three days then expands three times
- **Consequence**: Entire street's people die with bleeding eyes seven days later
