## 1. Kingdom of France (Valois)

### 1.1 Charles VII

- **Public Identity**: King of France, coordinates military and diplomacy at Nancy court
- **Action Style**: Calculated warfare, uses violence as policy tool
- **Resources**: Royal finances and nominal authority, siege leadership, marriage leverage
- **Hidden Agenda**: Use border actions to "create necessity" for 1445 standing army reform
- **Easy-to-Discover Secret**: Tolerates mercenary plunder for self-sustenance
- **Hard-to-Discover Secret**: Holds a "captain ransom and loot ledger"

### 1.2 Louis (Dauphin, Future Louis XI)

- **Style**: Pragmatic, suspicious, good at long-term games
- **Goals**: Secure throne, weaken potential rivals, build personal intelligence network

---

## 2. Holy Roman Empire and Habsburg

### 2.1 Emperor and Princes

- **Public Goals**: Defend imperial territory, maintain legal order
- **Hidden Motives**: Use foreign troops to restrain Swiss/princes
- **Assets**: Legalities, titles, prince network
- **Weaknesses**: Center has no troops or taxes; "inviting foreign troops" brings legitimacy damage

### 2.2 Free Cities like Strasbourg

- **Public Goals**: Autonomy, trade, city defense, freedom from extortion
- **Hidden Motives**: Use great power collision to raise negotiating price and tax rights
- **Assets**: Guild militia, walls, accounting rooms, diplomatic flexibility
- **Weaknesses**: Internal factional tears; grain prices and rumors can pierce order

---

## 3. Duchy Burgundy (Philip the Good)

- **Public Goals**: Border stability, trade protection, mediation
- **Hidden Motives**: Expand influence, connect north and south territories, buy future
- **Assets**: Funds, chancellor system, noble allies, knight order reputation
- **Weaknesses**: Can't let France advance east "take the chips"; can't let empire collapse to uninvestable
- **Methods**: Loans, marriages, guild sponsorship, cultural united front

---

## 4. Church Politics

### 4.1 Basel Council

- **Public Goals**: Defend orthodoxy, end schism, constrain heresy
- **Hidden Motives**: Use war as legitimacy touchstone
- **Assets**: Confession and absolution, monastery networks, jurisdiction, sanctuary rights
- **Weaknesses**: Schism discounts authority; any uncanny can backfire on its narrative

### 4.2 Clement VIII (Mist History Core)

- **Daylight History**: Abdicated 1429, died winter 1445/46
- **Mist History**: Formally submitted, soul unsubmissive, prayed to God "let the world witness the world without grace"

---

## 5. Fehm Court (Vehmgericht)

### 5.1 Public Image

- Secret trial system, handles "what public courts can't handle" — dignitaries and death sentences
- Symbols: Body in tree + dagger stuck in tree (carve S.S.G.G.)

### 5.2 Mist History Supplement

- Some executions are not political, but "Mist pollution screening"
- This makes it both terrifying and potentially "self-righteous"

---

## 6. "Flayer" Mercenary Consortium

- **Public Goals**: Money, ransom, winter camp supplies, legalization
- **Hidden Motives**: Through intimidating cities and parliaments, force out "sustainable protection fee system"
- **Assets**: Mobile violence, kidnapping network, loot cash flow
- **Weaknesses**: Mutual suspicion internally; implodes quickly once ransom channels lost

---

## 7. Cross-Domain Faction Agents

### 7.1 English Line

- **Agents**: Wool merchants, cloth brokers, Channel ship captains, exiled knights
- **Goals**: Maintain key trade nodes, compete for contracts
- **Secrets**: Debts, smuggling, double contracts

### 7.2 Lowlands and Merchant League

- **Agents**: Guild representatives, Merchant League tax officials, port notaries
- **Goals**: Reduce friction, stabilize exchange rates
- **Secrets**: Manipulate tariffs, monopolize storage

### 7.3 Mediterranean Line

- **Agents**: Marine insurance brokers, debt collectors, monastery messengers
- **Goals**: Control port franchises, acquire alum/spice contracts
- **Secrets**: Transport "undeclarable goods" (relic fragments/forbidden books/monster parts)
