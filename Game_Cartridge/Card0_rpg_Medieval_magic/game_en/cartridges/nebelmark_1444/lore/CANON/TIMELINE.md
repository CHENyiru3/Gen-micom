## Daytime History Main Timeline (Key Nodes)

### 1444
- **Treaty of Tours** (May): Hundred Years' War ceasefire, released mercenaries flood eastward
- **Crack Phase begins**: Weirdness first appears as rumors and individual cases at border
- **Council of Basel ongoing**: Church authority continues fragmenting
- **French royal revival**: Charles VII begins projecting power eastward
- **Burgundy expansion**: Philip the Good continues integrating northern and southern territories

## Mist History Timeline (Hidden Events)

### 1443 - Night Before the Rift
- **Clement VIII** dies (before winter solstice)
- Final plea: "Let the world witness: what a world without Grace looks like"

### 1443 Winter Solstice - Rift Born
- **Clement VIII** dies that night
- **Rift first appears on Temple Mount, Jerusalem**
- Father Marcus is present, witnesses colorless light and "something being born or dying" sounds

### 1444 - Crack Phase Spreads
- **Spring 1444**: Jerusalem rift briefly closes, then recurs
- **Summer 1444**: Rift appears in Alexandria, someone attempts blood sacrifice
- **Blood sacrifice aftermath**: Rift expands three times, entire street dies with bleeding eyes
- **Autumn 1444**: Rift appears in Constantinople
- **Around Winter Solstice 1444**: Blue light appears in Nebelheim quarry

## Historical Archives (Optional Background)

### Clement VIII (Gil Sánchez Muñoz y Carbón)

- **Life**: Became antipope after Pisa Council 1417, abdicated 1429, retired to Mallorca
- **Historical status**: Last abdicating antipope
- **Mist History role**: Final plea initiates Crack Phase

### Elijah

- **Research time**: Ten years studying rifts
- **Experience**: Jerusalem, Alexandria, and other locations
- **Current status**: Appears in Nebelheim

### Alexandria Failure Case

- **Time**: Spring 1444
- **Method**: Blood sacrifice (seven children)
- **Result**: Rift temporarily closes for three days, then expands three times
- **Consequence**: Entire street dies from bleeding eyes seven days later
